# Instruction: What is a Term?

Based on ISO 1087-1:2000 and IATE standards, a term is a verbal designation of a general concept in a specific subject field.

## Selection Criteria
1. **Domain Specificity**: The word or phrase must belong significantly to the chosen domain (e.g., Medicine - Celiac Disease).
2. **Noun Preference**: Terms are primarily nouns or noun phrases.
3. **Multi-word Units (MWUs)**: Include phrases like "gluten-free diet" or "small intestine biopsy" if they represent a single concept.
4. **Acronyms/Abbreviations**: Include domain-specific acronyms (e.g., "tTG-IgA").

## Exclusion Criteria
1. General vocabulary (e.g., "patient", "doctor", "hospital" - unless used in a specific technical sense).
2. Verbs or adjectives that do not form part of a fixed terminological unit.
3. Non-technical terms.

## Verification Checklist
- [ ] Does it represent a distinct concept?
- [ ] Is it frequently used in the corpus?
- [ ] Is there an equivalent in the target language?
