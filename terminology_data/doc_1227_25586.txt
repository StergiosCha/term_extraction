Short history of the Termbase ACOUTERM 
 
 
The database of acoustical terms ACOUTERM is quadrilingual (Greek, English, French and German terms) 
and was created in 1990 by the chairman of the Group ELOT/TC2/WG5, Kostas Valeontis, as a dBASE III-
Plus database, while in 1999 it was converted by him into an MS Access 97 database; today it is an MS 
Access 2000 database. 
The development, operation, maintenance and feeding of ACOUTERM has been performed by the chairman 
of ELOT/TC2/WG5, who has also the responsibility for the content in the website. 
Accessing and searching the termbase ACOUTERM is available on the Internet, via ELETO’s website, by its 
creator, who has already provided ELETO with glossaries of acoustical terms (English-Greek, Greek-
English) from the termbase. 
The field structure of the termbase ACOUTERM comprises the following basic fields (which, in each entry, 
correspond to a single concept):

Stage of processing 
 
Today, the termbase ACOUTERM contains 10.349 terminological entries of acoustics. 
Note 1 
Since there is no national language policy for establishing rules of creation and application purely Greek 
acronyms, initialisms and abbreviations, the English ones are generally used in Greek too, especially those 
that have been internationalized. 
Note 2 
Terms being in stage of processing 3 are definitive. Those in stage 2 have been prepared by 
ELOT/TC2/WG5 and the relevant source is in a Draft status for Public Enquiry. Stage 4 or greater indicates 
that the relevant term has been subjected to one or more revisions.
