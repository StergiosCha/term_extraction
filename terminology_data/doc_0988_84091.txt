Curriculum vitae 
Dr. Irine Darchia is Associate Professor in Classical Philology, Deputy Head of the Institute of Classical, 
Byzantine and Modern Greek Studies and Head of the Quality Assurance Service of the Ivane Javakhishvili Tbilisi 
State University. She defended her doctoral thesis on Plato’s Phaedo (Literature, Philosophy, Mythology, 
Mysticism) in 1998 and her post-doctoral thesis (Habilitation) on Colour Phenomenon in Greek Tragedy in  2005.  
She studied Modern Greek Language at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens and in the Summer 
School of Kalymnus organized by the Committee of International and Cultural Exchange of Athens-Kalymnus.  
She systematically participates in international conferences, seminars, workshops, trainings, projects concerning 
classical and modern Greek studies, educational issues and the Bologna Process (Georgia, Greece, Italy, 
Germany, Austria, Bulgaria, Russia, USA).  
She is teaching at the Departments of Classical, Byzantine and Modern Greek Studies and she was visiting 
professor at the Freie University of Berlin (Germany) and the University of Pisa (Italy).  
She speaks six foreign languages (Modern Greek, English, Russian, Italian, Ancient Greek, Latin), has published 
four books (two monographs, handbook, dictionary) and more then twenty articles on ancient Greek philosophy, 
tragedy, colour perception and the history of Greek language.
