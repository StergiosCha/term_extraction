EAFT–ELETO Symposium 
“National Languages and Terminology  
in Higher Education, Science & Technology” 
Athens, Greece, 7 November 2013 
 
Peep Nemvalts 
Born October 4, 1950 in Tallinn, Estonia 
1974 Tartu University, Estonia, MA in Estonian philology, 1976 doctoral student, Department of 
Estonian, Tartu University 
1991–1996 doctoral student, Department of Finno-Ugric Languages, Uppsala University, Sweden 
1996 PhD thesis “Case Marking of Subject Phrases in Modern Standard Estonian” published in 
English (Acta Universitatis Upsaliensis. Studia Uralica Upsaliensia 25. Uppsala 1996), was 
defended at Uppsala University in Swedish. A slightly modified version published in Estonian: 
Aluse sisu ja vorm. Alusfraasi käändevaheldus tänapäeva eesti kirjakeeles. Tallinn: Eesti Keele 
Sihtasutus, 2000. 
Has worked as research associate, lecturer, associate professor, senior researcher at universities in 
Estonia (Tallinn), Finland (Oulu), Sweden (Uppsala, Stockholm). 2008 he founded the Centre for 
Academic Estonian at Tallinn University, currently Head of the Centre. Has organised two national 
conferences on academic Estonian (2009, 2012) and an international conference on National 
Languages in Academic Research and Higher Education (2011). 
Main research interests: Estonian syntax and semantics, Estonian-Finnish contrastive studies, 
Estonian language planning, academic Estonian and terminology. 
Memberships: Emakeele Selts – Mother Tongue Society, Estonian Association for Applied 
Linguistics, Estonian Terminology Society, Johannes Aavik Society (chair of the Board), Finno-Ugric 
Studies Association of Canada (Member-at-Large of the Executive Committee 2006-2008).
