Short history of the Termbase GEOTERM 
 
The Termbase GEOTERM is bilingual (Greek and English terms) and was created, on April 2024, by Kostas 
Valeontis, president of the Hellenic Society for Terminology (ELETO) and chairman of ELETO’s Scientific 
Board (GESY) in order to import all the English and Greek equivalent terms of the dynamic English – Greek 
glossary of geological terms, which is the product of collaboration between ELETO and the Geological 
Society of Greece (GSG). This glossary has been compiled and is fed and maintained by Nicos Carras, 
ELETO–GSG liaison person. The new entries of English and Greek equivalent geological terms being 
inserted into the Glossary are previously submitted for comment to members of the two scientific societies 
and approved by the General Scientific Board (GESY) of ELETO. 
The development, operation, maintenance and feeding of the Termbase GEOTERM, as a database MS 
Access 2000, is performed by its creator himself, who has also the responsibility for the content of ELETO’s 
website, while searching via the Termbank of the website is being ensured by Nikos Valeontis, the IT 
administrator of the website. Thus, access and search in the Termbase GEOTERM is freely available on the 
Internet. 
The field structure of the Termbase GEOTERM comprises the following fields (in each entry, corresponding 
to a single concept), the first five of them being basic: 
FIELDS OF GEOTERM  
English term 
English abbreviation 
Greek term 
Greek abbreviation 
Code of source 
Code of term 
Foreign language reference source 
Greek language reference source 
Stage of processing 
Today, the Termbase GEOTERM contains 1363 terminological entries (with Greek and English equivalent 
terms) contained in the 16th edition of the Glossary.
