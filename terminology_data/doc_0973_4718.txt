Curriculum vitae 
Janet DeCesaris teaches Spanish-English and Catalan-English translation at PompeuFabra 
University in Barcelona, Spain, where she has been on the faculty since 1993. She has degrees 
in Spanish and Linguistics from Georgetown University and Indiana University (Ph.D., 1988).  
Early in her career, she worked mainly on Spanish and Catalan phonology and morphology, and 
also worked as a professional translator in Barcelona.  
After she joined PompeuFabra University, she became interested in sense differentiation in 
monolingual and bilingual dictionaries, and the relationship between dictionary representation and 
lexical structure.  
She is a founding member of the University Institute for Applied Linguistics at PompeuFabra 
University.  
She is the author of several articles on dictionaries, and her book, Investigación de gramática (co-
author Patricia V. Lunn, published by Heinle&Heinle (Boston), 1992, 2nd ed., 2007) is widely 
used in American universities in the training of future Spanish teachers.  
She is an active member of both the Dictionary Society of North America (DSNA) and the 
European Association for Lexicography (EURALEX).  
She organized the 2008 EURALEX congress in Barcelona, and is currently President of 
EURALEX (2012-2014).
