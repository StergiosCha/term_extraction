Curriculum Vitae 
Maria Koliopoulou has studied at the Department of German language and philology at the University 
of Athens (1998-2002). She received her Master’s Degree in Contrastive Linguistics and Language 
Variation from the Patras University in Greece (2006). She continued her studies at the same 
University to earn a PhD degree in linguistics, specialising in Contrastive Morphology in German and 
Modern Greek (2013).  
After completing her PhD studies, she had a research scholarship from DAAD (Deutscher 
Akademischer Austausdienst ‘German Academic Exchange Service’) and worked for a year as a 
postdoctoral researcher at the Bielefeld University in Germany (2013-2014).  
Since 2014 she holds a post-doctoral position in the Department of Translation Studies at Innsbruck 
University. She currently teaches terminology management and terminography. Her main research 
interests include morphology and terminology. She has published, among others, in the Journal of 
Greek Linguistics, Italian Journal of Linguistics and Languages in Contrast.
