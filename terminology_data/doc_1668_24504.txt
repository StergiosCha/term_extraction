Curriculum vitae 
Keisuke KASUYA is Professor of Sociolinguistics at the Graduate School of Language and 
Society in Hitotsubashi University (Tokyo, Japan).  
His research interests include the macro-sociolinguistics, the discourse analysis of linguistic 
representation, and the comparative study of linguistic ideologies. He co-edited Language, State 
and Power (1997) and What is Linguistic Imperialism? (2000)(both in Japanese).  
Among his publications are “Discourses of Linguistic Dominance: A Historical Consideration of 
French Language Ideology” (International Review of Education, Vol.47, Nos.3-4, July 2001), 
“L’Hégémonie linguistique ---- pouvoir organisateur du ‘consentement spontané’”(Impérialismes 
linguistiques ---- hiers et aujourd’hui, sous la direction de Louis-Jean Calvet et Pascal Griolet, 
INALCO/Edisud, 2005), and “Rethinking of the Concept of Language Community”(in Japanese, 
Gengo-Shakai, vol.1, 2007).
