Athens, Greece, 7 November 2013 
 
Marjeta Humar 
Marjeta Humar, PhD, Independent Humanities Specialist, is a researcher in the Terminological 
Section of the Fran Ramovš Institute of the Slovenian Language, the Research Centre of the 
Slovenian Academy of Sciences and Arts, Ljubljana, Slovenia. 
Marjeta Humar’s research focuses on Slovene lexicology, terminology and terminography. From 
1974 to 1991 she participated in the development and completion of four volumes of the 
Dictionary of Standard Slovene, published in 1975, 1979, 1985 and 1991. Since 1991, she has 
been working on Slovene terminology and terminological dictionaries. She organised 
international conferences on terminology and edited the collections of papers from the 
conferences Terminologija v času globalizacije (Terminology in the Time of Globalization 
(2004)), Nacionalni jeziki v visokem šolstvu. Zbornik prispevkov z Mednarodne konference 
Jezikovna različnost in nacionalni jeziki v visokem šolstvu (National Languages in Higher 
Education. Collected papers from the International Conference Language Diversity and National 
Languages in Higher Education (2009)), Terminologija in sodobna terminografija (Terminology 
and Modern Terminography (2009)). 
Marjeta Humar is the author, co-author and editor of  several normative terminological 
dictionaries with explanations and their equivalents in English, French, German and Italian 
(papermaking (1996), law (1999), mountaineering (2002), gemology (2005), geography (2005), 
theatre (2009), electronics, electrical engineering and telecommunications (2010), pharmacy 
(2011)). She is currently working on the following projects: urban, legal, music, general 
technical dictionaries and antonymy in Slovenian terminological dictionaries.
