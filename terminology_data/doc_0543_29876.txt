Athens, GREECE, 22-24 October 2009 
 
 
 
Katherina Psarra 
MSc, PhD, EurClinChem 
 
Curriculum vitae 
I was born and live in Athens, Greece. I received my first degree in Chemistry from the 
Kapodistrian University of  Athens, my MSc in Clinical Biochemistry from the University of 
Newcastle u. Tyne, UK, my PhD in Immunology from the Medical School, Kapodistrian 
University of Athens.  
Since 1983 I have been working in the Department of Immunology – Histocompatibility of 
Evangelismos Hospital, Athens in the laboratory of immunophenotyping. In 1987 we 
established the first flow cytometry laboratory in Greece covering almost all clinical 
applications of flow cytometry in a routine clinical setting, including immunology, hematology, 
oncology, gynecology, infections, neurology. I am also involved in the education of medical 
doctors, chemists, biologists, biochemists in flow cytometry and since 2003 I teach flow 
cytometry in the postgraduate courses of Clinical Chemistry and Laboratory Medicine of the 
University of Athens and I have acted as supervisor of  PhD, MSc and undergradute theses in 
flow cytometry. 
I have been a founding member of the Hellenic Society of Cytometry and member of the 
Board since 1997 as  a treasurer (1997-2006), and as President of the Society since 2006, 
member of  ISAC since 1991 and of ESCCA since 2006. 
I have also been member of the Board of the Greek Society of Clinical Chemistry – Clinical 
Biochemistry since 1999 and President of the Society since 2008 as well as editor of the 
Society Bulletin since 2002. 
I have been member of the organizing and scientific committees of several congresses, 
President of the 3rd Panhellenic Congress of Cytometry (2004) and the 6th Panhellenic 
Congress of Clinical Chemistry (2006).
