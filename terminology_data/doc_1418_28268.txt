Curriculum Vitae 
 
Theodoros Katerinakis was born in Chania, Crete, Greece and is an information analyst from the 
Dept of Informatics of Athens University of Economics and Business.  
He attended postgraduate courses in National Nuclear Research Center ‘Demokritos’ and 
Technical University of Crete.  
He was a partner in ministerial committees, researcher in RC-AUEB and Oracle Hellas, editor in 
conference proceedings and textbooks, and the author of three books. His research is published 
by Sage, Palgrave-McMillan, Routledge, LIT Verlag.  
He has served as a computer analyst in Hellenic Air Force (during Imia crisis), in the "Greek 
Pentagon" and as Greek representative in the USN in Souda Bay, receiving USN citation letters.  
He spent 10+ years as an organization/HR consultant and Head of the Electronic Document 
Management System in Bank of Chania.  
His multidisciplinary activity covers the scope of societies like Greek Computer Society, Hellenic 
Society for Operational Research, ELETO, Hellenic Management Association, Planetary Society, 
AAAS, INSNA, ACA.  
His research interests focus on aviation communication, linguistics, banking, human resources 
development and information theory. He holds an MSc in Communication Science (Drexel, 2009),  
a PhD in Aviation Communication (Drexel, 2014) and Adjunct Faculty for Greek Studies at Drexel 
University, in Philadelphia, USA. Webpage At:
