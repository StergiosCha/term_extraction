EAFT–ELETO Symposium 
“National Languages and Terminology  
in Higher Education, Science & Technology” 
Athens, Greece, 7 November 2013 
 
Jan Hoel 
President, European Association for Terminology (EAFT), senior advisor at The Language 
Council of Norway, where he inter alia coordinates the work of the Council’s Terminology 
service. 
Mr. Hoel holds an M.Phil. in modern languages (Nordic, English, Spanish) from the 
University of Oslo. 
He has worked as a university lector abroad, in-house translator, text revisor and head of EU 
translations in the Norwegian Ministry of Foreign Affairs and is employed at the Language 
Council as from 2000. In 1992 he worked for the Council of the European Union in Brussels 
as a lawyer‒linguist. 
He is a member of the steering group of the Nordic terminology forum Nordterm since 2003 
and headed the group in 2005‒2007. He is also a member of the Board of the Nordic 
Association for Lexicology, NFL, where he was president in 2011‒2013. In 2009‒2011 he 
headed the external steering group of Standards Norway’s project establishing the freely 
accesible Internet-based termbank SNORRE. 
His main professional interests are terminology and LSP, lexicography and language in 
society. He is in particular preoccupied with the contemporary impaired situation of national 
languages in higher education and in society at large.
